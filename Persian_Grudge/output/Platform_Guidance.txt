1. DRAFT2DIGITAL (D2D) — PRIMARY DISTRIBUTOR
================================================================================

D2D serves as the wide-distribution backbone, pushing to Apple Books, Kobo,
Barnes & Noble, libraries (OverDrive, BorrowBox), Scribd, Vivlio, and others
from a single dashboard.

FILE FORMAT REQUIREMENTS
------------------------
- Upload a clean .DOCX file (Microsoft Word or LibreOffice export).
- Page size: US Letter or 6×9 — D2D reformats automatically, but consistent
  source formatting prevents conversion artifacts.
- Use Word's built-in Heading styles (Heading 1 for part titles, Heading 2 for
  chapter titles). D2D uses these to generate the Table of Contents.
- Embed fonts only if using non-standard typefaces; otherwise let D2D's
  converter handle typography.
- Images: insert inline at 300 DPI, RGB color mode. Keep total file size under
  50 MB. Compress images with TinyPNG or similar before inserting.
- Do NOT include headers, footers, or page numbers — ereaders handle these.
- Front matter order: title page → copyright page → dedication → epigraph →
  table of contents → author's note / preface.
- Back matter order: acknowledgments → endnotes/bibliography → about the
  author → also by Michael Rodriguez → call-to-action (mailing list link).
- Insert D2D's "Universal Book Link" placeholder in back matter so each
  retailer shows the correct purchase link for the reader's platform.

METADATA ENTRY TIPS
--------------------
- Title: THE PERSIAN GRUDGE
- Subtitle: How Iran and America Became Mortal Enemies
  (Enter subtitle in the dedicated subtitle field, not appended to title.)
- Author name: Michael Rodriguez
- Series: Leave blank unless part of a planned series.
- Description: Write two versions —
    • Short (150 words): punchy hook + premise + stakes. Optimized for search.
    • Long (500–800 words): full blurb with HTML formatting (<b>, <i>, <br>).
  D2D allows HTML in descriptions. Use bold for pull-quotes and italics for
  praise/endorsements.
- BISAC categories (select up to 5):
    1. POLITICAL SCIENCE / International Relations / General
    2. HISTORY / Middle East / Iran
    3. POLITICAL SCIENCE / American Government / General
    4. HISTORY / United States / 21st Century
    5. POLITICAL SCIENCE / Geopolitics
- Language: English
- Publication date: Set to your planned launch date (or pre-order date).
- ISBN: D2D provides free ISBNs, or purchase your own through Bowker
  (recommended for professional branding — the publisher name appears as yours
  rather than "Draft2Digital").

PRICING STRATEGY ACROSS CURRENCIES
------------------------------------
Set a USD anchor price and let D2D auto-convert, then manually adjust key
markets for psychological pricing:

  USD:  $9.99  (ebook)  |  $18.99 (paperback)  |  $28.99 (hardcover)
  GBP:  £7.99           |  £14.99              |  £22.99
  EUR:  €8.99           |  €16.99              |  €25.99
  CAD:  $12.99          |  $24.99              |  $37.99
  AUD:  $14.99          |  $27.99              |  $42.99

- Price the ebook at $9.99 to qualify for 70% royalty on Amazon while
  signaling "serious nonfiction" (not a $0.99 impulse buy).
- If running a launch promotion, temporarily drop to $4.99 or $2.99 — never
  $0.99 for political nonfiction (damages perceived authority).

PRE-ORDER SETUP GUIDANCE
--------------------------
1. Upload a placeholder DOCX (even a title page only) to secure the listing
   at least 3–4 weeks before launch. D2D allows up to 12 months of pre-order.
2. Set the pre-order price $1–2 lower than launch price ($7.99 vs. $9.99) to
   incentivize early buyers. On Apple Books, all pre-order sales count toward
   Day 1 rankings — this is a major advantage.
3. Upload the final manuscript at least 72 hours before the release date to
   allow processing time across all channels.
4. Promote the pre-order link using D2D's Universal Book Link (books2read.com)
   — it auto-detects the reader's preferred retailer.
5. Coordinate pre-order announcements with your email list, social media, and
   any podcast/media appearances.

2. AMAZON KDP — DIRECT PUBLISHING
================================================================================

KDP SELECT CONSIDERATIONS: EXCLUSIVITY VS. WIDE DISTRIBUTION
--------------------------------------------------------------
KDP Select requires 90-day EXCLUSIVE enrollment in Amazon's ecosystem (no
sales on Apple, Kobo, B&N, etc.).

  RECOMMENDATION: DO NOT enroll in KDP Select for this title.

  Rationale:
  - Political history readers skew older and more diverse in their reading
    platforms — many use Apple Books, Kobo, or library apps.
  - Wide distribution maximizes discoverability across multiple ecosystems.
  - Kindle Unlimited (included with KDP Select) pays per page read at roughly
    $0.004–0.005/page — far less than a $9.99 sale at 70% royalty.
  - KDP Select's main benefit (KU visibility, free promotions) favors genre
    fiction more than serious nonfiction.

  Exception: If after 6 months of wide distribution sales are heavily
  concentrated on Amazon (>85%), consider a 90-day KDP Select experiment to
  test KU performance. You can always un-enroll and return to wide afterward.

A+ CONTENT RECOMMENDATIONS
----------------------------
A+ Content (formerly Enhanced Brand Content) is free for KDP authors and
appears below the book description on the Amazon product page. Use it to:

  1. "From the Author" module:
     - Author photo + 200-word personal statement connecting you to the topic.
     - Example: "I spent three years interviewing diplomats, intelligence
       officers, and scholars on both sides of this conflict..."

  2. Comparison table module:
     - Compare THE PERSIAN GRUDGE to related titles readers might know,
       highlighting what makes yours unique (narrative approach, new sources,
       contemporary relevance).

  3. Image + text module:
     - Use a timeline graphic (1953 coup → hostage crisis → nuclear deal →
       Soleimani → present) as a visual hook.
     - Include a map of the Middle East with key locations referenced in the
       book.

  4. Editorial review quotes:
     - Pull early reviews, endorsements, or blurbs and display them
       prominently with reviewer attribution.

  Note: A+ Content is only available on Amazon.com (US), Amazon.co.uk, and a
  few other Amazon marketplaces. Prepare content for at least US and UK.

CATEGORY SELECTION STRATEGY
-----------------------------
Amazon allows 3 categories at upload, but you can request up to 10 total by
contacting KDP Support after publication.

  Primary categories (select at upload):
  1. Kindle Store > Kindle eBooks > History > Middle East > Iran
  2. Kindle Store > Kindle eBooks > Politics & Social Sciences >
     Politics & Government > International & World Politics > Middle Eastern
  3. Kindle Store > Kindle eBooks > Politics & Social Sciences >
     Politics & Government > Specific Topics > Political Freedom

  Additional categories (request via KDP Support after publishing):
  4. Books > History > Middle East > Iran
  5. Books > Politics & Social Sciences > Politics & Government >
     International & World Politics > Diplomacy
  6. Books > History > Americas > United States > 21st Century

  Why this matters: Smaller categories are easier to rank in. A top-10
  position in "History > Middle East > Iran" is far more achievable than
  "History" overall, and the orange "Best Seller" tag carries the same weight
  regardless of category size.

KINDLE COUNTDOWN DEALS STRATEGY
---------------------------------
Available only if NOT enrolled in KDP Select? No — Countdown Deals are a KDP
Select feature. Since we recommend wide distribution, this tool is unavailable.

  Alternative promotion strategies on KDP without Select:
  - Temporary price drop to $4.99 for 5–7 days, coordinated with email blasts
    and social media.
  - Amazon Advertising (Sponsored Products campaigns) — target competitor
    titles and relevant keywords. Budget: $10–20/day during launch month.
  - Time price promotions to coincide with relevant news events (Iran nuclear
    talks, diplomatic incidents, anniversary dates of key events in the book).

PAPERBACK / HARDCOVER THROUGH KDP PRINT
-----------------------------------------
  Paperback:
  - Trim size: 6" × 9" (standard for nonfiction, good shelf presence).
  - Paper: white paper (better for books with images/maps) or cream paper
    (easier on the eyes for text-heavy reads). Recommendation: cream.
  - Cover finish: matte (signals literary/serious nonfiction).
  - Upload a print-ready PDF interior and a cover PDF using Amazon's cover
    template (includes barcode placement and spine width calculation).
  - Price: $18.99 (printing cost for ~350 pages at 6×9 cream is roughly
    $5.50, leaving ~$6.15 royalty at 60% after printing costs).

  Hardcover:
  - Available on KDP as case laminate (printed cover glued to boards).
  - Trim size: 6" × 9"
  - Price: $28.99 (printing cost is higher, ~$10–12, but hardcovers signal
    prestige and make excellent gifts).
  - Consider launching hardcover simultaneously with ebook, then adding
    paperback 3–6 months later at a lower price point.

  Expanded Distribution: Enable this in KDP Print settings to make the
  paperback available to bookstores and libraries through Ingram's network.
  Note: Royalties are lower (40% list minus printing cost), but it extends
  reach significantly.

3. APPLE BOOKS — VIA D2D OR DIRECT
================================================================================

APPLE BOOKS-SPECIFIC METADATA
-------------------------------
- Apple Books uses its own category taxonomy. When publishing via D2D, the
  BISAC codes you selected are automatically mapped to Apple's categories.
- If publishing direct via Apple Books for Authors (formerly iTunes Connect):
    • Category 1: Nonfiction > Politics & Current Events > International
    • Category 2: Nonfiction > History
- Apple Books supports rich HTML descriptions. Use formatting liberally:
  bold key phrases, add line breaks, include a "What you'll learn" section.
- Apple allows a "sample" of up to 20% of the book. Ensure Part 1 contains
  a strong hook — Apple readers frequently browse samples before buying.
- Add editorial reviews/blurbs if available — Apple prominently displays these.

PRE-ORDER ADVANTAGES ON APPLE
-------------------------------
Apple Books is the BEST platform for pre-orders because:

  1. All pre-order sales are credited to release day. If 200 people pre-order
     over 4 weeks, Apple counts all 200 as Day 1 sales — dramatically boosting
     your chart position on launch day.
  2. Apple allows pre-orders up to 12 months in advance.
  3. Apple features pre-orders in editorial collections and "Coming Soon" lists
     — free visibility before the book even launches.

  Action plan:
  - Set up the Apple pre-order (via D2D or direct) at least 4 weeks before
    launch.
  - Price the pre-order at $7.99 (vs. $9.99 launch price).
  - Drive as many pre-orders as possible to Apple specifically — share the
    direct Apple Books link with your audience segments who use Apple devices.
  - After launch, raise the price to $9.99.

4. KOBO — VIA D2D
================================================================================

KOBO PLUS ELIGIBILITY
-----------------------
Kobo Plus is Kobo's subscription reading service (similar to Kindle Unlimited).
When distributing via D2D, you can opt your title into Kobo Plus.

  RECOMMENDATION: Opt IN to Kobo Plus.

  Rationale:
  - Unlike KDP Select, Kobo Plus does NOT require exclusivity. You can be in
    Kobo Plus and sell on every other platform simultaneously.
  - Kobo Plus pays a per-read share from a monthly pool. The payout per borrow
    is typically lower than a full-price sale, but it provides incremental
    revenue and discoverability with no downside.
  - Kobo has a strong international presence (Canada, Australia, Netherlands,
    Japan, Belgium) — political nonfiction readers in these markets often
    discover books through Kobo Plus.

  To enable: In your D2D dashboard, look for the Kobo Plus toggle on your
  book's distribution settings page. Check the box. That's it.

PROMOTIONAL OPPORTUNITIES
---------------------------
- Kobo runs regular promotional events (Daily Deals, genre-specific sales).
  These are typically managed through D2D's promotions tab or by Kobo's
  merchandising team selecting titles from the D2D catalog.
- To increase chances of being selected:
    • Ensure cover art is high quality and genre-appropriate.
    • Keep your description polished and keyword-rich.
    • Participate in D2D promotional events, which often feed into Kobo's
      merchandising pipeline.
- Kobo Writing Life (direct dashboard) offers more granular promotional tools.
  Consider creating a direct Kobo account in parallel with D2D distribution
  for access to these tools — but only if you disable Kobo distribution
  through D2D to avoid duplicate listings.

5. BARNES & NOBLE — VIA D2D
================================================================================

NOOK PRESS CONSIDERATIONS
---------------------------
- D2D distributes to Barnes & Noble's Nook platform automatically. There is
  no need to create a separate Nook Press account unless you want direct
  control over B&N-specific promotions.
- B&N's ebook market share is small (~5–7%) but still worth serving. Their
  customer base skews toward readers who also buy physical books.
- Ensure your book description is clean and well-formatted — B&N's rendering
  engine handles HTML differently from other platforms. Test the listing after
  it goes live and contact D2D support if formatting looks off.
- B&N offers a "Free Friday" promotion for selected titles. While not suitable
  for a $9.99 political history, be aware of their "Nook Daily Find" deals at
  $1.99–$4.99 — D2D can submit your title for consideration.
- Physical bookstore placement: B&N stores stock print books through Ingram.
  Enabling KDP Print's Expanded Distribution or using IngramSpark gives your
  paperback/hardcover a chance of appearing in B&N's physical stores (though
  placement is never guaranteed without a traditional publisher or distributor
  relationship).

6. LAUNCH STRATEGY
================================================================================

WEEK 1-4 TIMELINE
-------------------

  WEEK 1 (Pre-launch: T-28 to T-21 days)
  ----------------------------------------
  □ Finalize manuscript — complete final proofread, format DOCX for D2D.
  □ Upload to D2D as a pre-order (placeholder file is fine).
  □ Upload to KDP as a pre-order (ebook + paperback + hardcover).
  □ Set pre-order price: $7.99 ebook / $18.99 paperback / $28.99 hardcover.
  □ Set up A+ Content on Amazon (draft all modules).
  □ Send ARC (Advance Reader Copy) to 20–30 target reviewers:
      - Book bloggers covering politics/history/geopolitics.
      - Journalists or academics in Middle Eastern studies.
      - Podcasters with relevant audiences.
      - Goodreads reviewers active in political nonfiction.
  □ Post "cover reveal" on social media (coordinate with Stage 3 assets).
  □ Email list announcement: "Pre-order now available" with links.

  WEEK 2 (Pre-launch: T-20 to T-14 days)
  ----------------------------------------
  □ Upload final manuscript to D2D and KDP (replace placeholder files).
  □ Begin Amazon Advertising campaigns (Sponsored Products):
      - Campaign 1: Target competitor book ASINs (similar Iran/US books).
      - Campaign 2: Target keywords ("iran history," "us iran relations,"
        "middle east politics," "persian gulf conflict").
      - Budget: $15/day, bid: $0.50–0.75 per click.
  □ Share pre-order excerpts on social media (quote graphics from Stage 3).
  □ Contact podcast hosts for launch-week interview slots.
  □ Follow up with ARC recipients — remind them to post reviews on launch day.

  WEEK 3 (Launch week: T-7 to T-0)
  ----------------------------------
  □ Final check: verify all listings are live and correctly formatted on every
    platform (Amazon, Apple, Kobo, B&N).
  □ Launch day email blast to full mailing list.
  □ Social media blitz (coordinated posts across platforms, using Stage 3
    graphics, video trailers, and quote cards).
  □ Request friends/family/colleagues post honest reviews on Amazon and
    Goodreads on Day 1 (aim for 10+ reviews by end of Day 1).
  □ Raise ebook price from $7.99 to $9.99 on Day 1 (pre-order discount ends).
  □ Publish A+ Content on Amazon (if not already live).
  □ Engage actively on social media — respond to comments, share reader posts,
    participate in relevant conversations about US-Iran relations.

  WEEK 4 (Post-launch: T+1 to T+7 days)
  ----------------------------------------
  □ Monitor sales data daily across all platforms.
  □ Adjust Amazon Advertising bids and keywords based on first-week data.
  □ Send follow-up email to anyone who opened but didn't click on launch email.
  □ Submit the book for editorial review sites with longer lead times:
      - Kirkus Indie (paid, ~$425, 7–9 week turnaround).
      - BlueInk Review (paid, ~$395).
      - BookLife by Publishers Weekly (free, 8–10 weeks).
  □ Begin planning first price promotion (2–3 weeks post-launch).
  □ Reach out to newsletter services for future promotions:
      - BookBub (submit for Featured Deal — long shot but highest ROI).
      - Written Word Media.
      - FreeBooksy/BargainBooksy.
      - Robin Reads.

REVIEW SOLICITATION APPROACH
------------------------------
Reviews are the single most important social proof for book sales. Strategy:

  1. ARC Distribution (pre-launch):
     - Use BookFunnel or StoryOrigin to distribute ARCs with built-in follow-up
       reminders asking for reviews.
     - Target 30 ARC recipients to realistically get 10–15 launch-day reviews.

  2. Amazon review requests (post-launch):
     - Include a gentle call-to-action at the end of the book: "If you found
       this book valuable, a brief honest review on Amazon helps other readers
       discover it."
     - Never incentivize reviews (violates Amazon TOS).
     - Ask your email list to leave reviews — but emphasize "honest" to stay
       compliant.

  3. Goodreads:
     - Create a Goodreads Author Profile if you don't have one.
     - List the book on Goodreads well before launch (it may already appear
       from Amazon/D2D metadata).
     - Run a Goodreads Giveaway (ebook giveaway is ~$119) to generate buzz
       and "to-read" shelving.

  4. Editorial reviews:
     - Even one Kirkus or PW review provides lifetime marketing material and
       credibility with bookstores and libraries.

PRICE PROMOTION SEQUENCE
--------------------------
  Launch day:       $9.99 (full price — maximize revenue from launch-day buzz)
  Week 3–4:         $4.99 (first promotion — coordinate with BookBub/newsletter
                     submissions, social media push, Amazon ad budget increase)
  Week 6–8:         $9.99 (return to full price)
  Month 3:          $2.99 (deep discount for 3–5 days, timed to a relevant news
                     event — e.g., anniversary of a key US-Iran incident,
                     nuclear talks, election cycle)
  Month 6+:         Rotate between $9.99 and $6.99 quarterly, testing which
                     price point maximizes revenue (not just units sold).

  Never permanently reduce below $4.99 for political nonfiction — it signals
  "remainder bin" to serious readers.

SOCIAL MEDIA COORDINATION WITH STAGE 3 DELIVERABLES
------------------------------------------------------
Stage 3 deliverables (graphics, video, social assets) should be deployed in
a coordinated cadence:

  Pre-launch (Weeks 1–2):
  - Cover reveal graphic (Instagram, X/Twitter, Facebook, LinkedIn)
  - "Coming soon" countdown graphics (1/week, then 1/day in final 3 days)
  - Author video: "Why I wrote this book" (60–90 seconds, for Instagram Reels,
    TikTok, YouTube Shorts)
  - Quote cards: 3–4 provocative passages from the book as shareable graphics

  Launch week (Week 3):
  - Launch announcement graphic with purchase links
  - Book trailer video (if produced) — share on all platforms
  - Daily quote cards (one per day for 7 days)
  - Behind-the-scenes content: research process, key discoveries, deleted
    chapters
  - Instagram/Facebook Stories: poll questions about US-Iran relations to
    drive engagement

  Post-launch (Week 4+):
  - Reader testimonial graphics (as reviews come in)
  - "Did you know?" fact graphics drawn from the book's content
  - Podcast appearance announcements with audiogram clips
  - Repurpose book content into Twitter/X threads (e.g., "The 7 moments that
    defined US-Iran hostility — a thread 🧵")

AMAZON CATEGORY HACK
----------------------
Amazon allows 7 keywords (actually keyword phrases, up to 50 characters each)
at upload. These keywords serve double duty: they help readers find your book
via search AND they can place your book in additional browse categories that
aren't available in KDP's standard category selector.

  Recommended keyword phrases:
  1. iran america history cold war middle east
  2. us iran relations hostage crisis 1979
  3. persian gulf geopolitics nuclear deal
  4. iranian revolution shah cia coup 1953
  5. soleimani assassination trump iran policy
  6. middle east conflict diplomacy foreign policy
  7. tehran washington political history nonfiction

  Category-triggering keywords:
  Some keyword phrases automatically place your book in specific categories.
  Research current triggers using Publisher Rocket or by checking Amazon's
  category strings. Examples that historically trigger useful categories:

  - "foreign policy" → may trigger: Books > Politics > International >
    Diplomacy
  - "cold war history" → may trigger: Books > History > Military > Cold War
  - "middle east politics" → may trigger: Books > Politics > International >
    Middle Eastern

  Important: Amazon's category-triggering keywords change periodically. Use
  Publisher Rocket ($199 one-time) or manually research by browsing Amazon
  category pages and noting the URL structure to identify exact category paths.

  After publication, request additional categories by emailing KDP Support at
  kdp-support@amazon.com:
  Subject: "Category addition request for ASIN [your ASIN]"
  Body: "Please add my book to the following categories: [list exact category
  browse paths]"
  Amazon typically approves 8–10 total categories.

SEASONAL RELEVANCE — GEOPOLITICAL EVENT HOOKS
-----------------------------------------------
Political nonfiction enjoys demand spikes tied to news events. Plan marketing
pushes around predictable dates and be ready to capitalize on breaking news:

  Predictable annual hooks:
  - November 4: Anniversary of the 1979 Iran hostage crisis.
  - January 3: Anniversary of the Soleimani assassination (2020).
  - March 20: Nowruz (Persian New Year) — cultural relevance hook.
  - August 19: Anniversary of the 1953 CIA coup in Iran.
  - September: UN General Assembly — Iran-related diplomacy often dominates
    headlines.
  - US election cycles: Iran policy is a recurring campaign issue.

  Breaking news hooks:
  - Nuclear negotiations progress or collapse.
  - Military incidents in the Persian Gulf or Strait of Hormuz.
  - Sanctions announcements or diplomatic overtures.
  - Protests or political upheaval in Iran.
  - Hostage or prisoner exchange events.

  Action plan for news hooks:
  1. Monitor Reuters, AP, and Al Jazeera for Iran-related breaking news.
  2. Within 24 hours of a major event, post social media content linking the
     news to your book's analysis (e.g., "I wrote about exactly this scenario
     in Chapter 12 of THE PERSIAN GRUDGE — here's what history tells us will
     happen next").
  3. Run a 48-hour price promotion ($4.99) timed to the news cycle.
  4. Boost Amazon Advertising spend by 3× for 72 hours targeting news-related
     keywords.
  5. Pitch op-eds or commentary pieces to media outlets, referencing your book.

  Keyword updates:
  - Revisit your Amazon keywords quarterly. If a new term enters public
    discourse (e.g., a specific treaty name, military operation, or political
    figure), swap it into your keyword list.
  - D2D keywords can also be updated at any time through the dashboard.
