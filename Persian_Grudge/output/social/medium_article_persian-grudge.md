# One CIA Coup, Seven Decades of Revenge: The Untold Story of Iran vs. America

In 1977, President Jimmy Carter stood in the Shah's palace in Tehran and called Iran "an island of stability." Twenty-three months later, sixty-six American diplomats were blindfolded and paraded before cameras. The American flag burned in the streets where Carter had raised his champagne flute.

From champagne to captivity in under two years. How does that happen?

The answer is both simpler and more disturbing than most people realize — and it starts not in 1979, but in 1953, when a CIA officer named Kermit Roosevelt (yes, Teddy's grandson) handed cash-stuffed envelopes to Iranian army officers in a Tehran basement and orchestrated the overthrow of the only democratic government Iran has ever known.

## The Coup That Created an Enemy

Most Americans start the clock on the Iran-US rivalry in 1979 — the hostage crisis, Khomeini's face on magazine covers, the humiliation of a superpower. Most Iranians start it in 1953. Both are right. Both are leaving out half the story.

In *The Persian Grudge: How Iran and America Became Mortal Enemies*, journalist Michael Rodriguez traces the full arc — from the discovery of oil beneath Persian soil in 1901 to the explosive Iran-Israel confrontation of 2024. What emerges is not a story of civilizational clash or religious war. It's a story of specific, documentable decisions made by specific, nameable human beings.

The pattern is almost mechanical: an outside power intervenes in Iran to secure its interests. The intervention works — for a while. Then it generates a backlash that is worse, more dangerous, and more ideologically committed than the original problem. The intervener responds with more intervention. Rinse, repeat, escalate.

The British wanted cheap oil in 1901. They got it. They also got the 1953 nationalization crisis. The Americans wanted a compliant anti-Communist ally. They installed one. They also got the 1979 Islamic Revolution.

## What You'll Discover

This isn't a 300-page opinion piece. Every claim is sourced. Every quote is verified. Rodriguez draws on declassified NSA documents, congressional testimony, and firsthand accounts to answer one question: *How did two countries with no territorial dispute, no ethnic conflict, and no real reason to be enemies end up locked in the most toxic rivalry in modern geopolitics?*

Here's what the book reveals:

- **The $480,000 deal that started everything** — how a bankrupt Australian adventurer bought oil rights across an area the size of France from a desperate Persian shah
- **Operation Ajax, minute by minute** — the CIA coup that toppled Mossadegh, told through declassified cables and the memoirs of the agents who ran it
- **The Shah's fatal miscalculation** — why a $100 million party in the desert became the symbol of everything Iranians hated about Western influence
- **The hostage crisis from inside the embassy** — what actually happened on November 4, 1979, told through the diplomats who were there
- **How America armed both sides** of the Iran-Iraq War — the bloodiest conflict of the late Cold War
- **The Soleimani assassination** — what it achieved, what it cost, and why the man who was killed was more important than most Americans understood
