COVER DESIGN BRIEF
===================
Title:    THE PERSIAN GRUDGE
Subtitle: How Iran and America Became Mortal Enemies
Author:   Michael Rodriguez
Genre:    Political History / Geopolitics / Nonfiction
Trim:     6 x 9 in (standard nonfiction)
Retailer: Amazon Kindle + print (must read at thumbnail)

Competitor references:
  - Stephen Kinzer, "All the Shah's Men" (sepia photograph cover)
  - David Crist, "The Twilight War" (dark military silhouette)
  - Trita Parsi, "Losing an Enemy" (clean white diplomatic look)
  - Kim Ghattas, "Black Wave" (bold red-and-black type)

Our covers must feel more urgent, more contemporary, and more
visually striking than any of the above at thumbnail scale.

========================================================================
CONCEPT 1 — SYMBOLIC / MINIMALIST
"The Knotted Flags"
========================================================================

VISUAL ELEMENT:
Two narrow vertical ribbons of fabric — one the stars-and-stripes of
the American flag, one the green-white-red of the Iranian flag — tied
together in a single, impossibly tight knot at the center of the cover.
The knot should look stressed, fraying at the edges, as though the
fabrics are about to tear. Below the knot, a few loose threads hang
down. The ribbons extend to the top and bottom edges of the cover,
giving a sense of tension pulling in opposite directions.

The background is a deep, matte charcoal-black — almost slate — with a
very subtle texture (linen or brushed concrete) so it doesn't feel
digitally flat.

COLOR PALETTE:
  - Background: charcoal black (#1C1C1E)
  - American ribbon: accurate flag colors (Old Glory Red #BF0A30,
    Old Glory Blue #002868, white)
  - Iranian ribbon: accurate flag colors (green #239F40, white,
    red #DA0000)
  - Title text: warm off-white (#F5F0E8)
  - Subtitle text: muted gold (#C9A96E)
  - Author name: same muted gold, smaller weight

TYPOGRAPHY:
  - Title "THE PERSIAN GRUDGE" in a condensed, all-caps serif with
    sharp terminals — think Didot Bold Condensed or Playfair Display
    Black. Large enough to fill the width of the cover. Kerning tight.
    Positioned in the upper third, above the knot.
  - Subtitle in a clean sans-serif (Neue Haas Grotesk or Helvetica Now
    Text Light), small caps, letterspaced +80, centered below the knot.
  - Author name at the bottom in the same sans-serif, regular weight.

THUMBNAIL TEST:
At thumbnail, the viewer sees: a dark rectangle, two colored streaks
meeting in a visible knot, and large white title text. The contrast
is immediate. The knot reads as conflict even at 80 px tall.

DESIGNER NOTES:
No photographs. No maps. No people. The entire emotional weight is
carried by the knot metaphor. This cover says: these two nations are
bound together by a grudge neither can untie. Consider a spot-UV or
foil treatment on the knot for the print edition.


========================================================================
CONCEPT 2 — PHOTOGRAPHIC / DOCUMENTARY
"The Burning Embassy"
========================================================================

VISUAL ELEMENT:
A full-bleed, high-contrast black-and-white photograph fills the
entire cover. The image evokes the 1979 US Embassy takeover in Tehran:
a crowd of protesters surging toward a compound wall, fists raised,
banners overhead. The photograph should feel archival — slightly
grainy, with crushed blacks and blown-out highlights, as though pulled
from a wire-service negative.

Over the top half of the photograph, a bold translucent wash of deep
blood-red (#8B0000 at roughly 60% opacity) bleeds downward like a
gradient, fading into the untouched black-and-white image below. The
title sits within this red zone.

At the very bottom of the cover, a thin horizontal strip (approx.
0.5 in) shows a contrasting modern color photograph — a tight crop of
the Iranian and American flags side by side on a negotiating table, or
two diplomats' hands almost touching across a table. This strip is in
full, saturated color, creating a jarring time-jump from 1979 to the
present.

COLOR PALETTE:
  - Photograph: true black-and-white, high contrast
  - Overlay gradient: deep blood-red (#8B0000) to transparent
  - Bottom strip: full color, desaturated just 10%
  - Title text: pure white (#FFFFFF)
  - Subtitle text: light warm gray (#D4CDC3)
  - Author name: white

TYPOGRAPHY:
  - Title in a bold slab serif — Rockwell Extra Bold or Sentinel Black.
    All caps. Stacked on two lines: "THE PERSIAN" / "GRUDGE" with
    "GRUDGE" significantly larger (150% scale) to create visual weight
    at the bottom of the title block.
  - Subtitle in a light sans-serif (Proxima Nova Light), sentence case,
    italicized, positioned just below the title.
  - Author name at the bottom, inside or just above the color strip,
    in small caps sans-serif.

THUMBNAIL TEST:
At thumbnail, the viewer sees: a dramatic dark image with a red wash,
large white slab-serif text, and a thin line of color at the bottom
that creates intrigue. The red-over-black-and-white reads as
"historical conflict" instantly.

DESIGNER NOTES:
Use royalty-free or public-domain archival imagery. If exact 1979
embassy photos pose rights issues, use a stylistically similar crowd
scene or composite. The grainy, analog quality is essential — this must
not look like a stock photo. Consider adding a barely visible datestamp
"1979" in the lower-left corner of the B&W section in a monospaced
typeface (Courier) as a subtle documentary detail.


========================================================================
CONCEPT 3 — TYPOGRAPHIC / BOLD
"The Cracked Title"
========================================================================

VISUAL ELEMENT:
The cover is almost entirely typography. The title "THE PERSIAN GRUDGE"
dominates 70% of the cover area, set in an extremely heavy, ultra-bold
condensed sans-serif (Impact, Bebas Neue Black, or custom hand-lettered
block letters). The letters are packed edge-to-edge with almost no
margins — they should feel like they are pressing against the cover
boundaries, claustrophobic and aggressive.

A single dramatic crack runs vertically through the center of the word
"GRUDGE," splitting the letters G-R-U | D-G-E. The crack is irregular,
jagged — like fractured concrete or broken stone. Through the crack, a
sliver of a photograph is visible: a strip of the Iranian landscape
(desert, mountains, or the Azadi Tower silhouette at sunset in warm
amber tones). This crack-and-glimpse effect is the only visual imagery
on the cover.

The background behind the letters is a flat, saturated navy blue
(#0A1628) — dark enough to feel serious, blue enough to feel political.

COLOR PALETTE:
  - Background: deep navy (#0A1628)
  - Title text: stark white (#FFFFFF) or very pale ice-blue (#E8EDF2)
  - Crack interior: warm amber/ochre photograph (#D4890F tones)
  - Subtitle text: amber gold (#D4890F)
  - Author name: white, 40% opacity

TYPOGRAPHY:
  - Title: ultra-bold condensed sans-serif, all caps. "THE PERSIAN" on
    line one, "GRUDGE" on line two, with "GRUDGE" at least 180% the
    size of "THE PERSIAN." The letters in "GRUDGE" should touch or
    nearly touch each other and the cover edges.
  - Subtitle centered below, in a refined serif (Garamond Premier or
    Cormorant Garamond) at a much smaller size — the contrast between
    the brutal title font and the elegant subtitle font is intentional.
    Sentence case, italicized.
  - Author name at the very bottom, sans-serif, small caps, letterspaced.
  - Optional: a one-line endorsement blurb at the very top in small
    italic serif, e.g., "A gripping history of the world's most
    dangerous rivalry."

THUMBNAIL TEST:
At thumbnail, the viewer sees: a dark blue field, enormous white block
letters, and a golden crack splitting the word. It reads as powerful,
confrontational, and high-stakes. The typography alone sells the book.

DESIGNER NOTES:
This concept lives or dies on the typography. Commission custom
lettering if budget allows. The crack must feel organic, not like a
Photoshop filter — hand-draw it or use displacement mapping on a real
concrete-crack texture. The amber landscape visible through the crack
should be subtle, not overpowering — it's a whisper of place inside
a shout of conflict.


========================================================================
CONCEPT 4 — SPLIT / CONTRAST
"Two Capitals"
========================================================================

VISUAL ELEMENT:
The cover is divided vertically into two exact halves by a thin,
razor-sharp line (1-2 px, metallic gold or white).

LEFT HALF — TEHRAN:
A moody, desaturated photograph of Tehran's skyline with the Milad
Tower (or Azadi Tower) visible. Color-graded in cool tones: teal,
slate blue, muted green. The sky is overcast or shows a sunset filtered
through smog. Overlaid with a very faint, ghostly watermark of Persian
calligraphy (a line from Hafez or Ferdowsi) at low opacity. The mood
is ancient, brooding, proud.

RIGHT HALF — WASHINGTON:
A similarly moody photograph of the Washington, D.C. skyline — the
Capitol dome or the Lincoln Memorial. Color-graded in warm tones:
amber, warm gray, desaturated gold. The sky mirrors the Tehran side
in composition (same cloud line, same horizon height) but differs in
color temperature. Overlaid with a faint watermark of the US
Constitution's "We the People" preamble at the same low opacity.

The two halves should be photographed or composed to feel like mirror
images — same scale, same horizon line, same level of detail — so the
viewer's eye immediately registers the comparison.

The title is centered across both halves, bridging the dividing line.
This is critical: the title literally spans the divide.

COLOR PALETTE:
  - Left half (Tehran): teal-blue-green tones (#1A3A4A, #2E5A5A, #4A7A6A)
  - Right half (D.C.): amber-gold-warm tones (#5A4A2E, #8A7A5A, #C4A46A)
  - Dividing line: metallic gold (#C9A96E) or crisp white
  - Title text: white (#FFFFFF) with a subtle drop shadow for legibility
    over the photographic backgrounds
  - Subtitle text: metallic gold (#C9A96E)
  - Author name: white, bottom center

TYPOGRAPHY:
  - Title in a strong, elegant serif — Freight Display Bold or
    Tiempos Headline Bold. All caps, generously letterspaced (+60).
    Centered vertically in the upper third, spanning both halves.
    "THE PERSIAN GRUDGE" on one line if it fits; otherwise stacked
    as "THE PERSIAN" / "GRUDGE."
  - Subtitle in a refined sans-serif (Neue Haas Grotesk Light), small
    caps, centered below the title.
  - Author name at the bottom, serif, regular weight.
  - Optional: "TEHRAN" in tiny letterspaced caps at the bottom of the
    left half, "WASHINGTON" in the same style at the bottom of the
    right half — as subtle geographic labels.

THUMBNAIL TEST:
At thumbnail, the viewer sees: a cover cleanly split into a cool blue
half and a warm gold half, divided by a visible line, with white text
bridging both. The split composition is unique among competitor covers
and immediately communicates duality, opposition, and two-sided
storytelling.

DESIGNER NOTES:
The mirror-image composition is everything. Spend time finding or
shooting photographs of both skylines at the same angle, same focal
length, same time of day. If exact matches aren't possible, use
artistic composites. The ghostly calligraphy / Constitution watermarks
should be visible only on close inspection — they reward the reader
who picks up the physical book. For the print edition, consider a
soft-touch matte laminate with spot-gloss on the dividing line and
title text.


========================================================================
GENERAL NOTES FOR ALL CONCEPTS
========================================================================

SPINE TEXT:
  All concepts should include spine design: title in the same typeface
  as the cover, author name below, publisher logo at the bottom. Spine
  background should match the dominant cover color.

BACK COVER:
  Brief synopsis (150 words), 2-3 endorsement blurbs, barcode area,
  publisher info. Background color and typography consistent with the
  front cover concept chosen.

EBOOK OPTIMIZATION:
  All concepts must be tested at 80 x 120 px (Amazon thumbnail) to
  ensure title legibility and visual impact. Avoid fine details that
  disappear at small sizes.

FILE DELIVERY:
  - Print-ready PDF at 300 DPI, CMYK, with 0.125 in bleed
  - Ebook cover as RGB JPEG/PNG, 2560 x 1600 px (Amazon KDP spec)
  - Social media assets: square (1080 x 1080) and story (1080 x 1920)
