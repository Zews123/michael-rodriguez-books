1. DRAFT2DIGITAL (D2D) — PRIMARY WIDE DISTRIBUTION
================================================================================

D2D Website: https://www.draft2digital.com

WHY D2D AS PRIMARY DISTRIBUTOR:
- Single dashboard to manage Apple Books, Kobo, Barnes & Noble, libraries,
  and 15+ additional retailers
- No exclusivity requirements
- Free formatting tools (D2D Book Formatter)
- Universal Book Links (Books2Read) included free
- Payment aggregation across all channels
- Print distribution via D2D Print (beta/expanding)

STEP-BY-STEP UPLOAD PROCESS:

  Step 1: Create Account
  - Sign up at draft2digital.com
  - Complete tax information (W-9 for US authors)
  - Set up payment method (PayPal or direct deposit)

  Step 2: Start New Title
  - Click "My Books" → "Add New Book"
  - Select "eBook" as format

  Step 3: Enter Metadata
  - Title: The Shadow Cabinet: Conspiracy, Power, and the Architecture
    of Hidden Control
  - Author: Michael Rodriguez
  - Series: (leave blank unless part of a series)
  - Description: Use the full book description from Publishing_Package.txt
    (2,000-character limit for some retailers; prepare a short version too)
  - BISAC Categories (select up to 3):
    * POL042000 — Political Science / Political Ideologies / Conspiracy Theories
    * HIS037000 — History / Modern / 21st Century
    * SOC026000 — Social Science / Sociology / General
  - Keywords: Enter 7 keywords/phrases (see Publishing_Package.txt)
  - Language: English
  - Publisher: [Your imprint name or leave as author name]
  - Publication Date: Set to your target launch date

  Step 4: Upload Manuscript
  - Accepted formats: .docx (preferred), .epub, .odt
  - Upload the final, proofread manuscript
  - Use D2D's free formatter to generate front/back matter
    (title page, copyright page, table of contents, About the Author)
  - OR upload a pre-formatted .epub if you have one

  Step 5: Upload Cover
  - Requirements: JPEG or PNG
  - Minimum 1600 x 2400 pixels (recommended: 2560 x 3840)
  - RGB color mode
  - File size under 10 MB
  - Must include title, author name, and be legible as a thumbnail

  Step 6: Set Pricing
  - Base price: $9.99 USD
  - D2D will auto-convert to local currencies for international stores
  - You can manually adjust per-territory pricing if desired
  - Royalty rate: Varies by retailer (typically 60-70% of list price)

  Step 7: Select Distribution Channels
  - CHECK ALL that apply:
    ✓ Apple Books
    ✓ Barnes & Noble / Nook
    ✓ Kobo
    ✓ Tolino (German market)
    ✓ Scribd
    ✓ Vivlio
    ✓ Palace Marketplace (libraries)
    ✓ Hoopla (libraries)
    ✓ Baker & Taylor (libraries)
    ✓ Bibliotheca (libraries)
    ✓ BorrowBox (libraries — UK/Australia)
  - SKIP Amazon (handle KDP separately for better control)

  Step 8: Review and Publish
  - Preview the formatted eBook in D2D's online viewer
  - Check all metadata is correct
  - Click "Publish"
  - Books typically go live within 1-3 business days per retailer

PRE-ORDER SETUP VIA D2D:
  - D2D supports pre-orders on Apple Books, Kobo, and B&N
  - Set pre-order 2-4 weeks before launch date
  - Upload a placeholder manuscript (can be partial)
  - Final manuscript must be uploaded at least 72 hours before release
  - Pre-orders accumulate and count toward first-day sales rankings
  - Apple Books is especially strong for pre-orders — set these early

FORMATTING REQUIREMENTS:
  - Use heading styles (Heading 1, Heading 2) for chapter titles
  - Include a linked table of contents
  - Page breaks before each new chapter
  - Do not use headers/footers (irrelevant for eBooks)
  - Avoid manual line spacing; use paragraph styles
  - Images must be embedded, not linked
  - Footnotes/endnotes: Use Word's built-in footnote tool
    (D2D converts these to pop-up notes automatically)

2. AMAZON KDP — DIRECT PUBLISHING
================================================================================

KDP Website: https://kdp.amazon.com

KDP vs. D2D FOR AMAZON:
  - ALWAYS publish directly on KDP for Amazon — do NOT use D2D for Amazon
  - Reasons:
    * Higher royalty (70% on KDP vs. ~60% through D2D's Amazon channel)
    * Faster publishing (24-72 hours vs. up to 2 weeks via aggregator)
    * Access to KDP Select / Kindle Unlimited (only available direct)
    * A+ Content and Amazon ads only available to direct KDP publishers
    * Better real-time sales reporting
    * Price matching and promotion control

KDP SELECT / KINDLE UNLIMITED — DECISION FRAMEWORK:

  WHAT IT IS:
  - 90-day exclusive enrollment in Kindle Unlimited (KU)
  - eBook must be exclusive to Amazon (no Apple, Kobo, B&N, etc.)
  - Readers with KU subscription can read for free; you earn per page read
  - Auto-renews every 90 days unless you opt out

  PROS FOR THIS GENRE (Political Nonfiction / Conspiracy Analysis):
  + KU readers consume nonfiction heavily — high page-read potential
  + Visibility boost in Amazon's algorithm during enrollment
  + Kindle Countdown Deals and Free Book promotions available
  + Good for building initial readership and reviews
  + Per-page-read income can exceed per-sale income for long books

  CONS FOR THIS GENRE:
  - Loses all non-Amazon distribution (Apple, Kobo, libraries)
  - This topic has a global audience — exclusivity limits reach
  - Library distribution is impossible during enrollment
  - You cannot sell direct from your website
  - 90-day lock-in periods

  RECOMMENDATION FOR "THE SHADOW CABINET":
  → START WIDE (skip KDP Select at launch)
  - This book's subject matter appeals to a global, privacy-conscious
    audience — many of whom deliberately avoid Amazon
  - Library distribution is valuable for credibility and discoverability
  - Apple Books and Kobo are strong for nonfiction internationally
  - If sales plateau after 6 months, consider a 90-day KDP Select test
  - You can always go exclusive later; going wide after exclusive
    means rebuilding from zero on other platforms

STEP-BY-STEP KDP UPLOAD:

  Step 1: Create KDP Account
  - Go to kdp.amazon.com and sign in with Amazon account
  - Complete tax interview (IRS W-9 or W-8BEN)
  - Set up bank account for royalty payments

  Step 2: Create New Title — Kindle eBook
  - Click "Create" → "Kindle eBook"
  - Language: English
  - Title: The Shadow Cabinet
  - Subtitle: Conspiracy, Power, and the Architecture of Hidden Control
  - Author: Michael Rodriguez
  - Description: Use HTML formatting for bold, italic, bullet points
    (Amazon allows basic HTML in descriptions — use it for readability)
  - Keywords: Enter 7 keyword phrases (max 50 characters each)
    * conspiracy theories political power
    * shadow government deep state
    * hidden power structures history
    * political manipulation democracy
    * secret societies influence
    * intelligence agencies covert operations
    * power architecture global politics
  - Categories: Select 2 BISAC categories (can add more via Author Central)

  Step 3: Upload Manuscript
  - Accepted: .epub (preferred), .docx, .kpf (Kindle Create output)
  - Use Kindle Previewer 3 to check formatting before uploading
  - Download: https://www.amazon.com/Kindle-Previewer/b?node=21381691011

  Step 4: Upload Cover
  - JPEG or TIFF only
  - Ideal: 2560 x 1600 pixels (1.6:1 ratio)
  - Minimum: 1000 x 625 pixels
  - Maximum file size: 50 MB
  - RGB color mode, 300 DPI for best quality

  Step 5: Pricing
  - Select 70% royalty plan (available for $2.99-$9.99)
  - Set US price: $9.99
  - Check "Automatically set my price based on the selected marketplaces"
    OR manually set international prices
  - Delivery cost: ~$0.06 per MB deducted from royalty (70% plan only)

  Step 6: Review and Publish
  - Do NOT enroll in KDP Select (keep wide distribution)
  - Review all details
  - Click "Publish Your Kindle eBook"
  - Book goes live in 24-72 hours

A+ CONTENT RECOMMENDATIONS:
  A+ Content (formerly Enhanced Brand Content) adds rich media to your
  Amazon product page. Available after your book is live.

  - Access via: Author Central → Marketing → A+ Content
  - Create a "Brand Story" module with:
    * Author photo and bio
    * Other books in your catalog
    * Your website/newsletter link
  - Create content modules:
    * Module 1: "What You'll Discover" — 3-column layout with key topics
      (e.g., Shadow Networks, Power Architecture, Democratic Implications)
    * Module 2: "Who This Book Is For" — target reader descriptions
    * Module 3: Testimonial/review quotes (once available)
    * Module 4: Comparison chart (if you have multiple books)
  - Use high-quality images with text overlays
  - A+ Content increases conversion rate by 3-10% on average

AMAZON AUTHOR CENTRAL:
  - URL: https://author.amazon.com
  - Set up BEFORE or immediately after book goes live
  - Add:
    * Professional author photo
    * Author biography (up to 2,000 characters)
    * Blog feed (link your blog/website RSS)
    * Video (book trailer if available)
    * Events (book signings, speaking engagements)
  - Claim all editions of your book (eBook, paperback, hardcover)
  - You can add up to 10 categories per book through Author Central
    (beyond the 2 allowed during KDP upload)
  - Monitor your Author Rank and Sales Rank via Author Central dashboard

3. APPLE BOOKS — VIA D2D
================================================================================

DISTRIBUTION METHOD: Through Draft2Digital (see Section 1)
  - Apple Books does accept direct uploads via iTunes Connect /
    Apple Books for Authors, but D2D is simpler for most indie authors
  - If you want direct access later: https://authors.apple.com

APPLE BOOKS-SPECIFIC METADATA TIPS:

  Description:
  - Apple Books displays longer descriptions well — use the full version
  - Formatting is limited but line breaks are preserved
  - Front-load the most compelling hook in the first 2 sentences

  Categories:
  - Apple uses its own category system (mapped from BISAC by D2D)
  - Verify your book appears in the correct Apple category after going live
  - Strong categories for this book on Apple:
    * Nonfiction → Politics & Current Events
    * Nonfiction → Social Science

  Pricing:
  - Apple takes a 30% commission (you receive 70% via D2D minus D2D's cut)
  - Net to author via D2D: approximately 60% of list price
  - Apple supports agency pricing — your set price is the retail price
  - Price at $9.99 to match Amazon

  Pre-Orders on Apple:
  - Apple Books is the BEST platform for pre-orders
  - Apple counts all pre-order sales on the release date
  - This can catapult a book into Apple's charts on launch day
  - Set pre-order at least 2-3 weeks before launch
  - Promote the Apple Books pre-order link to Apple-device users

  Apple Books Features to Leverage:
  - "Want to Read" — encourage readers to add your book pre-launch
  - Apple editorial features — well-designed covers and strong metadata
    increase chances of editorial selection
  - Apple Books is strong internationally (UK, Canada, Australia, EU)

4. KOBO — VIA D2D
================================================================================

DISTRIBUTION METHOD: Through Draft2Digital
  - Direct option: Kobo Writing Life (https://www.kobo.com/writinglife)
  - D2D is recommended unless you want Kobo-specific promotions

KOBO PLUS CONSIDERATIONS:

  What is Kobo Plus?
  - Kobo's subscription reading service (similar to Kindle Unlimited)
  - Available in select markets (Canada, Netherlands, Belgium, etc.)
  - Two tiers: Kobo Plus Read (eBooks) and Kobo Plus Listen (audiobooks)

  Should You Enroll?
  - YES — Kobo Plus does NOT require exclusivity
  - You can be in Kobo Plus AND sell on Amazon, Apple, B&N simultaneously
  - Enrollment is available through both D2D and Kobo Writing Life
  - Revenue is based on a per-page-read model from a shared fund
  - Downside: May cannibalize some full-price sales on Kobo
  - Upside: Increased discoverability and additional revenue stream

  RECOMMENDATION: Enroll in Kobo Plus via D2D
  - It's free to join, no exclusivity penalty
  - Particularly valuable for reaching Canadian and European readers
  - Political nonfiction performs well on Kobo in international markets

KOBO-SPECIFIC TIPS:
  - Kobo's audience skews toward serious readers — nonfiction does well
  - Kobo has strong library partnerships (OverDrive integration)
  - Kobo promotions are available to D2D-distributed authors
  - Ensure your EPUB is clean — Kobo is strict about formatting errors

5. BARNES & NOBLE — VIA D2D OR BN PRESS
================================================================================

DISTRIBUTION OPTIONS:

  Option A: Via D2D (Recommended)
  - Simpler management — everything in one dashboard
  - D2D handles formatting conversion for Nook
  - Slightly lower royalty than direct

  Option B: BN Press Direct
  - URL: https://press.barnesandnoble.com
  - Higher royalty rate (65% for $2.99-$9.99 vs. ~60% via D2D)
  - Direct access to B&N promotional tools
  - B&N-specific sales reporting
  - More work to manage a separate platform

  RECOMMENDATION: Use D2D unless B&N becomes a significant revenue channel
  - Start with D2D for simplicity
  - If B&N sales exceed 15-20% of total revenue, consider switching to direct

NOOK-SPECIFIC CONSIDERATIONS:
  - Nook market share is small but loyal — dedicated Nook readers buy often
  - B&N's online store features "Nook Daily Find" promotions
  - Cover quality matters — B&N's website displays covers prominently
  - B&N has physical bookstore integration for print editions
  - B&N allows longer book descriptions than Amazon — use the space
  - Categories are BISAC-based — your D2D categories will map correctly

RECOMMENDED STRATEGY:
  1. KDP Print — for Amazon paperback and hardcover sales
  2. IngramSpark — for bookstore, library, and non-Amazon retail distribution
  3. Use the SAME ISBN for both platforms (they'll recognize it and avoid
     creating duplicate listings — see ISBN section below)

TRIM SIZE RECOMMENDATIONS:
  - Paperback: 6" x 9" (most common for nonfiction trade paperback)
  - Hardcover: 6" x 9" (standard nonfiction hardcover)
  - Why 6x9: Professional appearance, comfortable to hold, cost-effective
    printing, standard shelf size for bookstores
  - Alternative: 5.5" x 8.5" (slightly smaller, slightly cheaper to print)

PAPER TYPE:
  - Cream/Creme paper (not white) — industry standard for nonfiction prose
  - White paper — only for books with color images or heavy graphics
  - Paper weight: 60# (KDP standard) or 55# (IngramSpark standard)
  - Recommendation: Cream paper, standard weight → professional nonfiction look

COVER SPECIFICATIONS:
  Paperback Cover:
  - Full wrap (front + spine + back) as single PDF
  - Spine width calculated by: (page count × 0.0025") for cream paper
  - KDP and IngramSpark both provide cover calculators/templates
  - Bleed: 0.125" on all sides
  - Resolution: 300 DPI minimum
  - Color space: CMYK
  - Barcode: KDP adds automatically; IngramSpark — add your own or let them
  - Matte or glossy laminate finish (matte is more popular for serious
    nonfiction — choose matte)

  Hardcover Cover:
  - Case laminate (printed cover wrapped around boards) — most common
  - Dust jacket option available on IngramSpark
  - KDP hardcover: case laminate only, no dust jacket
  - Recommendation: Case laminate for both KDP and IngramSpark — simpler,
    more durable, no jacket to lose

INTERIOR FORMATTING:
  - Upload as PDF (preferred for print) or .docx
  - Margins: Minimum 0.5" on all sides (0.75" recommended for gutter/spine)
  - Font: 11pt or 12pt serif (Garamond, Palatino, or Georgia)
  - Line spacing: 1.15 to 1.3
  - Chapter title pages: Start on right-hand (recto) page
  - Page numbers: Bottom center or bottom outside corner
  - Headers/footers: Book title on left page, chapter title on right page
  - Front matter order: Half title, title page, copyright, dedication,
    table of contents, introduction
  - Back matter order: Acknowledgments, notes/references, bibliography,
    about the author

ISBN CONSIDERATIONS:

  Option A: Free KDP ISBN
  - Amazon provides a free ISBN for KDP Print editions
  - Imprint listed as "Independently Published"
  - CANNOT be used on any other platform (Amazon-only)
  - Fine if you only want to sell print on Amazon

  Option B: Purchase Your Own ISBN (RECOMMENDED)
  - Buy from Bowker (US): https://www.myidentifiers.com
  - Single ISBN: $125 | Pack of 10: $295 | Pack of 100: $575
  - Your imprint name appears as publisher
  - Use across ALL platforms (KDP, IngramSpark, D2D Print)
  - Required if you want IngramSpark distribution
  - More professional appearance

  ISBN ALLOCATION FOR THIS BOOK:
  - You need separate ISBNs for each format:
    * eBook (EPUB): 1 ISBN (optional — most eBook stores don't require it)
    * Paperback: 1 ISBN
    * Hardcover: 1 ISBN
    * Audiobook: 1 ISBN (if produced later)
  - RECOMMENDATION: Buy a 10-pack from Bowker ($295)
    Assign ISBNs to paperback and hardcover immediately
    Keep remaining ISBNs for future formats and future books

  ASIN NOTE:
  - Amazon assigns its own ASIN for the Kindle eBook
  - You do NOT need an ISBN for the Kindle edition
  - KDP Print requires an ISBN (use your own or Amazon's free one)

PRE-LAUNCH: 4 WEEKS BEFORE RELEASE
──────────────────────────────────────
□ Finalize manuscript — complete all editing and proofreading
□ Finalize cover design — all formats (eBook, paperback, hardcover)

□ Order proof copies (paperback and hardcover) for final review
□ Set up pre-orders on Apple Books and Kobo via D2D

──────────────────────────────────────

□ Upload paperback and hardcover to KDP Print
□ Upload to IngramSpark (if using)
□ Upload to D2D with all distribution channels selected

□ Create Books2Read universal link (free via D2D)
□ Set up Amazon Author Central profile
□ Email your existing mailing list — "Book coming in 2 weeks"
□ Send advance review copies (ARCs) to:
  - Book bloggers in political nonfiction / conspiracy analysis space
  - Podcast hosts who cover similar topics
  - Journalists covering power, politics, and media
  - Goodreads reviewers in relevant groups
□ Request early reviews — aim for 10-20 reviews by launch day
□ Schedule social media posts for launch week
□ Prepare launch day email to mailing list
□ Set up Amazon Ads campaign (start on launch day — see below)
□ Create Goodreads author profile and add book listing
□ Reach out to newsletter swap partners (BookFunnel, StoryOrigin)

──────────────────────────────────────
□ Verify all editions are live on all platforms:
  - Amazon (Kindle, paperback, hardcover)

  - Kobo
  - Barnes & Noble
  - Library channels
□ Send launch day email to mailing list
  - Include direct purchase links for ALL platforms
  - Ask for reviews — provide specific instructions
  - Share a compelling excerpt or key insight from the book
□ Post on all social media channels

  - Facebook: Post in relevant groups (political books, nonfiction readers)

□ Reach out to podcast hosts for interview opportunities
□ Monitor sales dashboard hourly on launch day (then relax)
□ Respond to any early reviews or reader feedback publicly

──────────────────────────────────────

Week 3:

8. PRICING STRATEGY
================================================================================

EBOOK PRICING: $9.99
──────────────────────────────────────
  Why $9.99:
  - Maximum price for Amazon's 70% royalty tier ($2.99-$9.99)
  - Standard price point for serious nonfiction (signals quality/depth)
  - Below the $12.99-$14.99 range of Big Five publishers → competitive
  - Above the $0.99-$4.99 range that signals "light" or "impulse" content
  - Sweet spot where readers expect substantive, well-researched work

  Royalty Breakdown at $9.99:
  - Amazon KDP (70%): $6.99 per sale (minus ~$0.06 delivery cost)
  - Apple Books via D2D: ~$5.99 per sale
  - Kobo via D2D: ~$5.99 per sale
  - B&N via D2D: ~$5.99 per sale

PAPERBACK PRICING: $17.99
──────────────────────────────────────
  Why $17.99:
  - Competitive with trade paperback nonfiction ($16.99-$19.99 range)
  - Enough margin above print cost for reasonable royalty
  - Estimated print cost (6x9, ~350 pages, cream): ~$5.50-$6.00
  - KDP Print royalty at $17.99: approximately $4.80-$5.20 per sale
  - Feels like a "deal" compared to $19.99 (psychological pricing)

  If the book exceeds 400 pages:
  - Print cost increases — consider pricing at $18.99 or $19.99
  - Use KDP's royalty calculator to ensure at least $4.00 net per sale

HARDCOVER PRICING: $27.99
──────────────────────────────────────
  Why $27.99:
  - Standard range for nonfiction hardcovers ($25.99-$32.99)
  - Signals prestige and collectibility
  - Estimated print cost (6x9, ~350 pages, case laminate): ~$10-$12
  - KDP Print hardcover royalty at $27.99: approximately $4.80-$5.60
  - IngramSpark hardcover: Higher margin possible with 40% trade discount

  Note: Hardcover is a prestige format — sales volume will be lower
  than eBook and paperback, but it legitimizes the title and appeals
  to libraries, gift buyers, and collectors.

LAUNCH PROMOTION PRICING:
──────────────────────────────────────
  Strategy: Launch at full price, then run targeted promotions

  Launch Day through Week 1:
  - eBook: $9.99 (full price — capture early buyers at maximum royalty)
  - Do NOT discount at launch — you want maximum revenue from your
    most excited audience (mailing list, followers, pre-order converters)

  Week 2-3 (First Promotion):
  - Consider a 48-72 hour eBook price drop to $4.99 or $5.99
  - Announce via email list and social media
  - Time this with any BookBub or newsletter features
  - Goal: Spike sales volume → improve Amazon ranking → organic discovery

  Week 4+:
  - Return to $9.99
  - Run periodic 48-hour sales ($6.99) every 4-6 weeks
  - Time sales with relevant news events or cultural moments
    related to the book's themes (political events, revelations, etc.)
  - Never go below $3.99 for nonfiction — protects perceived value

  Holiday Pricing:
  - Black Friday / Cyber Monday: $4.99 eBook, bundle with other titles
  - End of year: $6.99 "Best Books" promotion
  - Pair discounts with "gift this book" messaging for hardcover

  KDP COUNTDOWN DEAL (if enrolled in KDP Select):
  - Available once per 90-day enrollment period
  - Gradually steps price back up (e.g., $2.99 → $4.99 → $6.99 → $9.99)
  - You keep 70% royalty even at reduced prices (unique to Countdown Deals)
  - Can be very effective for visibility spikes

  PRICE MATCHING WARNING:
  - Amazon will price-match if your book is cheaper elsewhere
  - Keep prices consistent across ALL platforms
  - If running a D2D promotion, also adjust KDP price simultaneously
  - Amazon may drop your price to $0.00 if a free version is found
    anywhere online — monitor this
